A Refinement of Interval Approximations for Fully Commutative Quivers

Yasuaki Hiraoka, Ken Nakashima, Ippei Obayashi, Chenguang Xu

Abstract

A fundamental difficulty in multiparameter persistent homology is the absence of a complete and discrete invariant. To address this challenge, we propose a structuralized approach for investigating the representation of a fully commutative quiver. Our framework interprets a representation through interval subquivers and synthesizes these interpretations to arrive at a comprehensive understanding. We evaluate this framework on both finite-type and infinite-type commutative ladders, uncovering new insights that deepen our understanding of multiparameter persistent homology. In the finite-type cases, we discover an efficient method for the indecomposable decomposition leveraging solely one-parameter persistent homology. In the infinite-type scenarios, we introduce a novel invariant called a connected persistence diagram that exposes partial information about persistence pertaining to the second parameter via connecting two ordinary persistence diagrams using interval approximations. To broaden the applicability of our new methods, we then present several models for constructing commutative ladder filtrations. Employing these models, we reveal new properties of random filtrations and showcase the efficacy of the new invariant on point cloud data by analyzing the topology of crystal structures.
